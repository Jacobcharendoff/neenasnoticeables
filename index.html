I'm a maker in Toronto. I'm blind, so everything I create — my soaps, my candles, my little keepsakes — is made by feel and by scent. My hands are very picky. If a piece doesn't feel right, it doesn't leave my table.

a little note from me

Dear friend,

I make things you notice with more than your eyes. I lost most of my sight, and my hands quietly took over the work my eyes used to do. Honestly? They do it better. Every soap I cut and every candle I pour gets judged by feel, by weight, by scent. My hands are the quality control, and they are very hard to please.

I started making these for the people I love. Then their friends started asking. Then workplaces started asking. It turns out people are a little tired of gifts that come off a shelf, and a little hungry for things made slowly, by an actual person, at an actual kitchen table.

If you'd like me to make something for your people, I would truly love that.

Neena xo
