my story

Dear friend,

When my sight faded, everything else got louder.

The weight of a good bar of soap. The way lavender smells different in the morning than it does at night. The little sound a match makes the moment it catches. Most people rush right past these things. I can't anymore — and honestly, I wouldn't want to. Losing my sight didn't take my senses away. It turned the rest of them all the way up.

That's what a "noticeable" is. It's the thing you'd miss if you weren't paying attention. My hands learned to cut soap by weight and feel. My nose learned to blend a candle the way some people mix colours. Every piece I make gets judged by touch and scent before it leaves my table, and my hands are very hard to please.

So why gift bags? Because you can't explain noticing to someone. You have to hand it to them. Every bag I make is a little tour of the world the way I experience it — something for your hands, your nose, your tongue, your ears. And the candle's glow is the part I can't have, so that one is yours.

I started making these for the people I love. Then their friends started asking. Then workplaces started asking. It turns out people are a little tired of gifts that come off a shelf, and a little hungry to slow down and notice something real.

If you'd like me to make something for you or your people, I would truly love that.

Neena xo
