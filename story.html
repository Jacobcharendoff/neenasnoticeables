my story

Dear friend,

I make things you notice with more than your eyes. I lost most of my sight, and my hands quietly took over the work my eyes used to do. Honestly? They do it better. Every soap I cut and every candle I pour gets judged by feel, by weight, by scent. My hands are the quality control, and they are very hard to please.

I didn't set out to start a business. I started making these for the people I love. Then their friends started asking. Then workplaces started asking. It turns out people are a little tired of gifts that come off a shelf, and a little hungry for things made slowly, by an actual person, at an actual kitchen table.

Losing your sight takes a lot of things from you. But it gave me something too: it made me notice everything else. The weight of a good bar of soap. The way lavender smells different in the morning. The little details most people rush past. That's what I put into everything I make — and it's why I called this Neena's Noticeables.

If you'd like me to make something for you or your people, I would truly love that.

Neena xo
